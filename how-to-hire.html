How to Hire Filipino Workers

Understanding the legal, structured, step-by-step overseas hiring sequence mandated by Philippine regulations.

The Official 7-Step Sequence

To hire compliant and legally protected talent from the Philippines, overseas employers must follow this standardized sequence. We guide you through each milestone.

1

Employer Preparation

The journey must begin internally. We help you review your organizational readiness, ensure job descriptions match acceptable DMW guidelines, and gather the legal prerequisites needed to apply for accreditation.

2

Employer Accreditation

Before interviewing anyone, your company must be recognized as an accredited foreign employer by the Department of Migrant Workers (DMW). This requires verification of your corporate documents at the nearest Migrant Workers Office (MWO).

3

Job Order Approval

With an accredited status, your precise hiring demands (job titles, salaries, quotas) are submitted as a Job Order. Once approved, you legally gain the authorization to fill those positions.

4

Recruitment and Selection

Sourcing, candidate screening, interviews, and final selection happen here. This is legally conducted with the support of your tied Philippine recruitment agency.

5

Worker Documentation Processing

Candidates undertake rigorous processing including medical clearances, securing visas, attending pre-departure seminars (PDOS), and authenticating their own professional documents.

6

Final Deployment Clearance

The DMW reviews the applicant's processed file. Upon successful verification, the candidate is issued an Overseas Employment Certificate (OEC) which acts as an exit clearance.

7

Worker Deployment

Worker departs the Philippines and safely arrives at your global destination, completely authorized and legally protected.

Common Causes of Recruitment Delays

  • Interviewing Before Accreditation: Finding the perfect candidate only to realize government accreditation will take 6-8 weeks, causing the talent to find another job.
  • Mismatched Job Titles: Writing job titles on contracts that do not strictly match the DMW approved classification manual.
  • Incomplete Legal Documents: Submitting business registration or commercial licenses without the proper diplomatic authentication (Apostille).
  • Sub-standard Salary Offers: Attempting to offer packages below the host country minimums or the Philippine government’s established benchmarks.

Why Preparation Must Come First

Philippine deployment protocols are uniquely protective of its citizens. The system operates on an "accreditation-first" policy rather than a "hire-first" policy.

When overseas employers attempt to side-step preparation and jump straight into headhunting, they trigger massive administrative failures. PRVS ensures you operate in the exact chronological sequence demanded by the government, leading to friction-free hiring.
